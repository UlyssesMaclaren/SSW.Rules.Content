<span class='intro'> <p>​There needs to be consistency between all of your developers' timesheets, so you should get them all to adhere to the following&#58;</p><ul><li>Be accurate. Read <a href="/Management/Rules-to-Better-Timesheets/Pages/how-to-describe-the-work.aspx">Do you know how to describe the work you have done?​</a>
   <dl class="bad"><dt class="greyBox">'Fixed Bug on customer form'</dt><dd>Figure&#58; Bad example</dd></dl><dl class="good"><dt class="greyBox">'Investigated and improved the  validation on customer form to enable saving when Customer Name is &gt; 100 characters' </dt><dd>Figure&#58; Good example</dd></dl></li><li>Record all the work you do for a client, even if it is to be written off</li><li>If you are working with another employee, ensure your times are consistent, in both time and category</li><li>If you are providing telephone support, count your time in 15 minute blocks</li><li>If you work for a client 4 times in a day for 15 minutes each, feel free to enter one timesheet for 1 hour, listing the 4 things you did. The extra information adds no value and only serves to clutter up&#160;invoices</li></ul> </span>
